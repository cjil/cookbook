# Scones

## Recipe Details
- Servings: 12
- Difficulty: Easy
- Prep time: 10 minutes
- Cook time: 12-15 minutes
- Tags: baking, breakfast, tea, snack
- Categories: Baking, Breakfast

## Preamble
A simple, classic basic scone recipe adapted from Taste Australia. These scones are tender, golden, and made with only a few ingredients.

## Ingredients
- 450 g self-raising flour
- 80 g butter, chilled and cubed
- 250 mL milk, plus extra if required
- Plain flour, for dusting
- Jam, to serve
- Whipped cream, to serve

## Method
1. Preheat the oven to 200°C. Sift self-raising flour into a large bowl.
2. Using your fingertips, rub the butter into the self-raising flour until the mixture resembles breadcrumbs.
3. Make a well in the centre. Add 1 cup (250 mL) of the milk. Mix with a flat-bladed knife until the mixture forms a soft dough, adding more milk if required.
4. Lightly dust a flat baking tray with plain flour.
5. Pat the dough into a 2 cm-thick round. Using a 5 cm round cutter, cut out 12 rounds. Press the dough together and cut out the remaining rounds. Place the scones onto the prepared baking tray, about 1 cm apart. Sprinkle the tops with a little plain flour.
6. Bake for 12–15 minutes or until golden and well risen. Transfer to a wire rack to cool slightly.

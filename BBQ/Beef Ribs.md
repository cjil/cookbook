# Beef Ribs — Konnected Joe (Kamado)

## Recipe Details
- Servings: 4–6
- Difficulty: Intermediate
- Prep time: 20 minutes
- Cook time: 6–8 hours
- Tags: beef, ribs, kamado, Konnected Joe
- Categories: BBQ, Beef

## Preamble
Beef short ribs or plate ribs benefit from low-and-slow cooking on a Kamado to break down connective tissue and develop a rich bark. Use a robust beef rub from `BBQ/Rubs/`.

## Ingredients
- 2–4 beef short ribs or a slab of beef ribs
- Your favourite beef rub

## Method (Konnected Joe — Kamado)
1. Setup: use a plate setter or heat deflector for indirect cooking and a drip pan. Stabilize at 107–121°C. Add hickory or oak for a stronger smoke.
2. Trim excess fat, apply a binder, and coat ribs with rub. Rest 30 minutes to overnight.
3. Place ribs bone-side down and smoke at 107–121°C until the internal temperature approaches 90–95°C and the meat is probe-tender; expect ~6–8 hours depending on size.
4. (Optional) Wrap in butcher paper at the stall to speed through and retain moisture.
5. Rest 20–60 minutes before slicing between bones and serving.

## Notes
- Use a leave-in probe for consistent results. Beef ribs can take longer than pork ribs — rely on tenderness rather than time alone.

# Pulled Pork — Konnected Joe (Kamado)

## Recipe Details
- Servings: 6–10 (depending on shoulder size)
- Difficulty: Intermediate
- Prep time: 20 minutes (+ overnight rub rest if desired)
- Cook time: 8–16 hours (size-dependent)
- Tags: pork, pulled pork, kamado, Konnected Joe
- Categories: BBQ, Pork

## Preamble
Pulled pork made on a Kamado-style cooker like the Konnected Joe develops deep smoke flavor and tender, shreddable meat. Use your favourite pork rub from `BBQ/Rubs/`.

## Ingredients
- 1 Boston butt / pork shoulder (3–5 kg)
- Binder (yellow mustard or oil) — optional
- Your favourite pork rub

## Method (Konnected Joe — Kamado)
1. Setup: indirect configuration with a plate setter/heat deflector and a drip pan. Use lump charcoal with apple, cherry, or hickory wood chunks for smoke. Stabilize at 107–121°C.
2. Trim excess fat, apply a thin binder, and coat the shoulder with your rub. Rest 1–24 hours for deeper flavor.
3. Place the shoulder on the cooker fat-side up. Maintain 107–121°C and avoid opening the dome.
4. Cook until the internal temperature hits ~71–77°C — this is the stall. Optionally wrap tightly in foil or butcher paper and return to the cooker.
5. Continue cooking until the internal temperature reaches 90–96°C and the meat is easily pulled with a probe or forks.
6. Remove, rest wrapped for 1–2 hours (or longer in a cooler), then shred and dress with sauce or serve as desired.

## Notes
- Expect 1.5–2 hours per pound as a rough guide, but always verify doneness by tenderness and internal temperature. Keep vents small on the Konnected Joe for steady temps.

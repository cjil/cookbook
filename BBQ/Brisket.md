# Brisket — Konnected Joe (Kamado)

## Recipe Details
- Servings: 6–8
- Difficulty: Intermediate
- Prep time: 30 minutes (+ overnight rest if desired)
- Cook time: 10–16 hours (depends on size and temp)
- Tags: brisket, kamado, Konnected Joe, beef, low-and-slow
- Categories: BBQ, Beef

## Preamble
Cooked low and slow on a Kamado-style cooker like the Konnected Joe, brisket develops deep smoke flavor and a tender, sliceable bark. Use your favorite beef rub (see `BBQ/Rubs/Standard.md` or other rubs in `BBQ/Rubs/`).

## Ingredients
- 1 whole packer brisket (3–5 kg)
- Binder (yellow mustard or oil) — optional
- Your favorite beef rub

## Method (Konnected Joe — Kamado)
1. Setup: place a heat deflector or plate setter for indirect cooking and a drip pan beneath the grate. Use lump charcoal and small chunks of oak, hickory, or fruitwood. Aim to stabilize the cooker at 107–121°C.
2. Trim the brisket as desired, apply a thin binder, then liberally apply your favorite rub. Let rest 1–24 hours refrigerated if time allows.
3. Place the brisket fat-side up on the grill grates. Close the dome and maintain 107–121°C. Resist opening the lid — check only when necessary.
4. Smoke until the brisket reaches an internal temperature of ~71–77°C. The brisket will often hit a stall here.
5. Wrap the brisket tightly in butcher paper or foil (Texas crutch) and return to the cooker.
6. Continue cooking until the internal temperature reaches 90–95°C and a probe slides in with little resistance (probe tenderness over exact temp).
7. Remove, rest wrapped for 1–2 hours (or longer in a cooler), then slice against the grain.

## Notes
- Times vary with brisket size and cooker stability — plan for variability. Small adjustments to vents control temp on the Konnected Joe; use a leave-in probe thermometer if possible.

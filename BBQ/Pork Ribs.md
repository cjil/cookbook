# Pork Ribs — Konnected Joe (Kamado)

## Recipe Details
- Servings: 2–4
- Difficulty: Easy
- Prep time: 15 minutes
- Cook time: 4–6 hours
- Tags: pork, ribs, kamado, Konnected Joe
- Categories: BBQ, Pork

## Preamble
Tender, smoky pork ribs from a Kamado-style cooker benefit from low, steady heat and a flavourful rub. Use your favourite rub from `BBQ/Rubs/`.

## Ingredients
- 1 rack pork ribs (baby back or St. Louis style)
- Your favourite rib rub
- Optional: apple juice or cider for spritzing; brown sugar (if you prefer a sweeter finish)

## Method (Konnected Joe — Kamado)
1. Setup: arrange a heat deflector/plate setter for indirect heat and a drip pan. Stabilize at 107°C. Add fruitwood (apple or cherry) for subtle smoke.
2. Remove membrane, apply a light binder, then coat ribs with your favourite rub. Let rest 30 minutes to overnight.
3. Place ribs bone-side down on the grill and close the dome. Maintain 107°C.
4. Cook for ~3 hours unwrapped, spritzing with apple juice every 45–60 minutes if desired.
5. Wrap ribs tightly in foil with a small splash of apple juice (optional) and return to the cooker for 1–1.5 hours until tender.
6. (Optional) Unwrap and place back on the grill for 10–20 minutes to set a glaze or firm the bark; baste with barbecue sauce during this final stage if desired.
7. Rest 5–10 minutes, then slice between the bones and serve.

## Notes
- Times vary by rib type and thickness. Baby backs cook faster than St. Louis style ribs. Keep the lid closed on the Konnected Joe to hold steady temperature and smoke.
